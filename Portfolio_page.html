CHART 12 | Week 8: A history of UK base rate and inflation rate since 1989.

At the Bristol Festival of Economics Jagjit Chadha, Director of the National Institute of Economic and Social Research said that the last time the Bank of England tripled the base rate was in 2018. This is true and is shown by the increase in the grey line before collapsing to 0.1% as the Bank of England implemented expansionary monetary policy to support the economy during the Pandemic. The chart also shows the UK inflation rate in the blue line which the Bank of England targets to hit 2%. Although, interest rates did triple in 2018 this was by no means contractionary monetary policy as by historical standards the base rate is still at an all time low since the GFC. Therefore, even if the central bank were to raise interest rates in response to inflation reaching 4.2% we have to consider if the policy is expansionary or contractionary given the historical perspective.
